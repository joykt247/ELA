Frequently Asked Questions

Below are a list of Frequently Asked Questions about the OLS (Online School) This is the platform students need to log into everyday to see their schedule, join Class Connect links, and access their class Content, Progress, and Grades. and the ELA (English Language Arts) This is the class we will be working on our reading, writing, and creative skills. 7 course. You can find the answer to YOUR question by clicking on the plus sign (+) to the left of the question. There is a screenshot to show you exactly what your screen will look like and easy steps below to access the needed content. If you don't see your question answered here email Ms. T (jtersigni@ohva.org) for clarification!

 

How do I access my ELA online textbook?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Content from the top purple ribbon

  3. Locate the lesson you want to access in the left Table of Contents

  4. Click on the selected lesson

  5. Click Read to open link to the online textbook

How do I access previous lessons covered in ELA class?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Content from the top purple ribbon

  3. Locate the Search Topics box

  4. Type the lesson number into the box and hit Enter or the Search Icon

  5. Click into selected lesson

How do I check my ELA grades?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Grades from the top purple ribbon

How do I view turned in attempts of my ELA work?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Progress from the top purple ribbon

  3. Click Quizzes

  4. Click on the lesson number of the attempt you want to view

  5. Click the little down arrow ˅next to the title of the attempt

  6. Click Submission

  7. Click Attempt

How do I read feedback my teacher leaves me?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Grades from the top purple ribbon

  3. View written feedback from teacher in the Comments and Assessments column

When are assignments due?

Source: learning.k12.com

  1. Make sure you are logged into your English Course

  2. Click Plan from the top purple ribbon

  3. Hover your mouse over a pink bubble to see due dates for individual assignments

What happens when a unit closes?

  • A unit closes 2 weeks after we take the unit test on a Friday at 5 PM

  • Students may use this 2 week window to complete any overdue work from the unit

  • Students may use this 2 week window to review and reattempt any work for the unit

  • Once a unit is closed it will not reopen and grades for that unit are final
